Settings guide

How to choose black img settings for a useful result

The strongest black image is usually built from small decisions rather than one vague dark prompt. Use the table below to choose settings based on the job: product hero, poster background, architectural mood board, social graphic, or clean text-safe cover.

When the sample matters Upload a sample when the crop, silhouette, or object placement is already close. Keep the prompt short and describe what should become black, what should stay readable, and where the copy should sit. This works best for product shots, room renders, packaging, and poster drafts.
When texture matters Choose Silk for soft editorial backgrounds, Graphite for matte product contrast, Glass for sharper highlight lines, and Marble when the black img needs natural veins or a premium surface. Texture should support the subject instead of hiding it.
When light matters Use NW or NE light for a calm hero image, SE for a brighter commercial edge, and SW for a heavier poster mood. If the center becomes too dark, raise brightness before changing the prompt; if it becomes gray, lower brightness and switch to a tighter light angle.
When copy matters Keep overlay copy short. A black image for typography needs negative space, visible shadow detail, and fewer competing highlights. The prompt pack explicitly asks the model to reserve clean space so text does not fight the background.
When export matters Use the local PNG export for mood checks, client direction, and prompt review. Use paid server-side AI generation only when the Worker has provider keys, PayPal checkout, and Turnstile verification configured, so the generated black img follows the same settings contract safely.

A black img generator creates dark images with controlled light, texture, contrast, and optional text. The goal is not pure black, but readable black visuals for products, posters, architecture, and backgrounds.
